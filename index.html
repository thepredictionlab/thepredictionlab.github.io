Hightlight

One of our projects is to predict harmful algal blooms in detroit lake. Harmful algal blooms are a naturally occuring phenomenon, but they can impact the health of people and pets, and water managers benefit from early warning of a bloom. We develop state-of-the-art machine learning algorithms to predict the occurence of harmful algal blooms in Detroit Lake, Oregon; the main source of drinking water for the city of Salem. Our algorithms and their results are visualized in a routinely updated blog. This work was picked up by the press, and we even had a pod-cast about the project!

Predicting Harmful Algal Blooms

City of Salem Part 1.

Over the period Aug-2018 to Mar-2019, we worked with the City of Salem to develop prediction algorithms for harmful algal blooms (HABs). HABs occur in Detroit Lake, the current sole source of drinking water for the city and the surrounding region. Using data collected from the lake, we constructed a Bayesian Modeling Averaging framework for making 7-day predictions for the lake. This prototype prediction system was deployed for the 2019 HAB season. Our results were visualized in a public-facing blog.

  • Date: Aug 2018 - Mar 2019:
  • Client: The City of Salem
  • Category: Environmental Prediction
